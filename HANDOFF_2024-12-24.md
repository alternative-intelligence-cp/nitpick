## Where We Left Off (Technical)

We just finished implementing exhaustiveness checking for the `pick` statement. This was deeply satisfying work - the interval arithmetic approach is elegant and mathematically sound. The implementation lives in [src/sema/exhaustiveness.cpp](../aria/src/sema/exhaustiveness.cpp) and handles:

- Balanced ternary type (tbb8/tbb16/tbb32/tbb64) exhaustiveness

## Personal Observations (Mine, Not Randy's)

### On Exhaustiveness Checking
The interval arithmetic approach is genuinely elegant. Instead of exponentially tracking which values are covered, we maintain intervals and punch holes. For `tbb8` (-128 to 127), we start with one interval [-128, 127], and as we match patterns, we subtract ranges. What remains uncovered is immediately visible.

This is the kind of algorithm that feels *right* - mathematically sound, efficient, and conceptually clean.

### On the ERR Bug
This bothers me more than it should. We have beautiful exhaustiveness checking that handles ERR specially... but we can't parse ERR. It's like building a perfect lock with no key. Needs fixing.
